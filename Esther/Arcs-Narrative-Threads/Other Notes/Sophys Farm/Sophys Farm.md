- [x] Wurmple, Cascoon/Silcoon, Dustox/Beautifly
    -  **Wurmple**: Produces a coarse, low-quality white silk. Not particularly strong or luxurious but its readily available and easily dyed. The most numerous. Feeds off tree leaves & sap.
    -  **Cascoon**: Produces a soft, higher-quality silk with high luster and texture. Difficult to dye without ruining the quality of the silk.
     -  **Silcoon**: Produce a sturdy white silk that can be readily dyed and sold. Superior in quality to Wurmple silk.
     -  **Beautifly/Dustox**: Newly evolved Beautifly/Dustox (those that don’t stay to have children) fly off in large swarms, much like the Butterfree migrations. Massive migrations occur around Sept-Oct as the swarm avoids winter.
